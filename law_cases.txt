Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala (1973) – Established Basic Structure Doctrine: Parliament cannot alter the basic structure of the Constitution.
Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India (1978) – Expanded Article 21: Procedure established by law must be just, fair and reasonable.
Indira Gandhi v. Raj Narain (1975) – Election disputes and scope of judicial review; Indira Gandhi's election declared void.
S. R. Bommai v. Union of India (1994) – President’s Rule subject to judicial review; reinforced federal structure.
Minerva Mills v. Union of India (1980) – Struck down amendments violating Basic Structure Doctrine.
A. K. Gopalan v. State of Madras (1950) – Preventive detention under Article 22; early case on personal liberty.
Shreya Singhal v. Union of India (2015) – Struck down Section 66A IT Act; strengthened freedom of speech.
I. R. Coelho v. State of Tamil Nadu (2007) – Judicial review of constitutional amendments; limits on Parliament power.
K. S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India (2017) – Recognized Right to Privacy as fundamental under Article 21.
